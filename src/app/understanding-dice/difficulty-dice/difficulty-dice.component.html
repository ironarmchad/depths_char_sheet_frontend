Difficulty Class

In the case of player versus environment, it is often the case that the ST will just create a difficulty class (DC) for the action. The player will roll their stat and if their roll is greater to or equal to the Difficulty Class then they succeed. Let us look at the probabilities to determine what makes a reasonable difficulty class.

{{ chart }}

By inspecting the probability we can see that setting the DC of +2 to the stat or less results in a "don't Botch" situation, where the only failure is rolling minimum value. Setting a DC of +4 to stat sets up a 50/50 chance of success or failure. Setting a DC of +6 to stat allows only critical successes. Armed with this information, I use the 50/50 chance to set DCs with this question: What type of person has a 50/50 chance of success? If the normal human does, I set DC based on normal human stat value of 3, giving us a DC of 7. If you need to be superhuman to achieve a 50/50 chance then perhaps base the DC on 7, giving us a DC of 11.
